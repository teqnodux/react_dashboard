Merger Background Analysis - Client Deliverables
Chronological Summary
TXNM's Board periodically reviewed M&A opportunities and entered into a merger agreement with Avangrid in October 2020, which Avangrid terminated in January 2024, after which the Board continued reviewing strategic alternatives including a possible sale.
• TXNM conducted a targeted auction from December 2024 to April 2025, contacting five infrastructure funds with all five signing NDAs, three submitting first-round bids, and one submitting a second-round bid.
• Party E submitted an unsolicited $60/share bid on February 28, 2025 requesting 30-day exclusivity but never meaningfully engaged after receiving dataroom access on March 12, 2025.
• The Board selected infrastructure funds over strategic buyers based on financial resources, interim financing capability, competitive cost of capital, regulatory approval track record, reduced transactability risk, and lack of strategic buyer interest in the prior process.
• Two parties submitted final bids: Blackstone Infrastructure (infrastructure fund with perpetual capital structure) at $60.25/share as submitted, subsequently negotiated to $61.25/share prior to signing, and Party A (infrastructure fund requiring passive minority investors) at $60.25/share before withdrawing.
• The Board selected Blackstone's $61.25/share all-cash bid as the highest offer with superior financial strength, perpetual capital structure enabling long-term ownership, stakeholder commitments including local management and workforce continuity, and strong regulatory approval track record.
• Regulatory considerations differentiated bidders, with Blackstone offering a $350 million reverse termination fee for regulatory failure and demonstrating a strong approval track record, while Party A raised significant concerns about regulatory covenants and Party D presented additional regulatory execution risk.
• The merger agreement includes no financing condition, with Blackstone providing debt commitment letters at signing and closing a $400 million PIPE investment at $50/share on June 2, 2025, fifteen days after signing.
• Process rumors leaked to press on March 6, 2025, with Bloomberg publishing reports on March 10-11, 2025 and March 18, 2025, generating inbound inquiries from strategic and financial investors that the Board declined to pursue.
• Blackstone proposed $58/share on February 18, 2025, increased to $61/share on April 14, 2025, and finalized at $61.25/share on April 18, 2025. The final $61.25/share offer exceeded Blackstone's initial $58/share proposal by $3.25/share, representing a 5.6% increase and characterizing the final round as an up-bid overall.
